instructions:
  - title: Prepare the pesto
    steps:
      - Note that you can also use nettle leaves, carrot tops, radish tops or wild herbs as a base instead of basil leaves. The same goes for pine nuts that can be replaced with any kind of seeds or nuts you have on hand.
      - Soak your basil leaves in cold water for 10 minutes. This optional step slows down the leaves oxidation, avoiding bitterness.
      - Tare your food processor, add the pine nuts and blend until the nuts are very finely chopped.
      - Lift the basil from the bowl of water, shake the excess water off, and add it to the food processor. Check the combine weight of pine nuts and leaves.
      - Add 2–2.5% of the weight of your ingredients in salt. For example, for 400 g of leaves and pine nuts, add approximately 10 g of salt (when in doubt, always round up your salt quantity).
      - Lightly blend, and let it sit for about 15 minutes.
      - Some brine should have been released from your leaves. Transfer everything to a tightly sealed jar. Use a weight to keep the greens underwater.
  - title: Store the pesto
    steps:
      - Store in a cupboard for 3–7 days, then in a cold room for three weeks — or depending on the sourness you want to achieve. It will keep for months in the refrigerator.
      - Before serving, you can mix in some olive oil, parmesan cheese, garlic, or anything your pesto might be lacking.
faq:
  - question: How long can I keep this fermented pesto?
    answer: Once ready, this pesto can be kept for months in the refrigerator.
  - question: Why can't I add the oil and cheese from the start?
    answer: It is important to only add the cheese and olive oil when you are ready to consume the pesto, as their high-fat content would cause it to spoil.
