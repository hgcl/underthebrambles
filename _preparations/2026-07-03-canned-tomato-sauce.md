instructions:
  - title: Prep your sauce
    steps:
      - Wash, trim, and halve tomatoes. Cut the other vegetables if using.
      - Add the tomatoes to a big pot, and start simmering as soon as possible. Reducing the sauce takes a lot of time, you can add more tomatoes to the pot as you cut them.
      - When all tomatoes are added to the pot, blend to your liking.
      - Add the optional dry herbs and cut vegetables to the pot.
      - Simmer until reduced to the desired thickness.
      - Add salt to taste.
      - (Optional) Add citric acid to taste, and to improve spoilage prevention.
  - title: Seal and sterilize your jars
    steps:
      - While the sauce cooks, clean and heat jars in boiling water.
      - Fill each jar with hot sauce, leaving 1.5 cm headspace.
      - Debubble, wipe rims, add lids.
      - Sterilize your jars in a pressure cooker (30 min from the whistle) or in a regular pot with boiling water (40 min).

Pasta with tomato sauce is probably the quintessential easy dish in Western Europe. Yet just a few hundred years ago, tomatoes were unknown in the Mediterranean region. It was only after the Spanish colonized the Americas that the _pomo d'oro_ — literally "golden apple" — made its way across the Atlantic.

In 1692, Italian chef Antonio Latini published what is considered the earliest known Italian recipe for tomato sauce. Its name? The "Spanish tomato sauce".

As for the first recipe for preserved tomato sauce, it appeared in 1795 in Vaucluse, in southern France.

Sometimes the oldest recipes are also the simplest. With ripe tomatoes and a little patience, you can make a sauce worthy of its long history.
